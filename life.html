|Everything is Just a Story


Life is not always what we expected to be so we have to value every moment before it turns into memory.

Hi everyone. On the 16th day of March 2002, a boy came to exist which fulfilled the wish of his parents to have a son. That’s me! I am Julecenio Estorco, sweet 16 with 3 years of experience. Enough to be engaged in the reality of life. I came from Canlaon City Negros Oriental but currently resides here at Talamban Cebu to pursue my greatest goal. I am the youngest in the family. I have four sisters, three of them have already settled down with their own family. My mother is Amparo Estorco and my father is Julecenio Angana. By the way, my father made me his junior but I don’t bring it as a suffix for some reasons. Maybe you get confused why I am bringing my mother’s surname rather than my father’s.

Well, the history of my family is kind of different. My parents had different hometowns and because of financial instability, my mother was forced to find work and coincidentally, she found work as a housemaid for the sister of my father. My father’s family owned land before and they were privileged in life. So that's it, my parents met but the thing was, my father wasn't a young man anymore that time. He was married but separated yet gifted with two kids. His wife left them and went somewhere. So yah, we are just the second family, and maybe not maybe, yes, that's the reason why my father's family’s treatment of us isn’t nice until now.

Going back, so my parents decided to build their own family, against all odds as they say. My father accepted my mother and my mother accepted my father as well as his kids. My mother told me that we were financially stable before, even though our family grew bigger but my father managed a land as his part from his family. After giving birth to their fourth child which is also a girl, somehow my parents lost their hope to have a boy then maybe accidentally, in a peaceful, romantic night where two souls collided with the same wish of having a baby boy, they made me. Then my mother gave birth to me, they were very happy. But everything has its opposite. That happiness equates grief. Or maybe I came to exist as someone will come to an end.

After six months since I was born, my father passed away. I don’t get to see him. I don't even have a photograph of him. Sometimes, I ask myself whether I inherit his appearance, his height, his physique or anything. Whenever I see a child with his father, I feel envious. Like I just wish my father was still with us. I just wanna experience being piggybacked with a father, playing some sports with him, talking with him man to man, going out with him and being with him until I reach my dreams. I just want him to see me grow and become the man he wants me to be. I just want to fulfil his dreams for me but sadly, it won’t happen anymore as we are living in two different worlds now.

After that, after my fathers death, our state just became tough. My half-brother started to squander my father’s land. My mother just tolerated it as she didn’t want to be in an argument. My mother became a full time father and a mother to us. She started to work in others' farms, washed clothes in our neighbors, and did anything just to support our needs. My sisters had to go to school at least twice a week to raise our family. Worse was, the people we expected to help us, turned their back against us, my father’s family but it’s okay, as in the first place, they never treated us as family. It was very hard for my mother’s side yet she endured everything for us. Until I grew up and witnessed her struggles. But then again, she managed to plaster a curve in her face. She loved us, she loved me more. I’m her favorite among us siblings, she gave me more in everything and my whole life just depended on her. When I woke up in the morning, everything was all set. I was free. She filled the gaps in everything my father failed to do. I may not have told her how much I love her and how much grateful I am that she's my mother, but God knows how my heart wants to tell her those words. But I just too late to realize such thing. Now she’s gone and that’s my greatest and biggest regret in my life so far, to not be able to tell her “I love you, Ma”.

Truly, life goes on. I thought that if I get wasted, what will be the reaction of my mother seeing her boy messed up with life? Besides, I still have my sisters that truly love me unconditionally. They kept on telling me that they are just on my side no matter what. I learned to accept that God has the reason why it happened. And I think I already know it, for me to become independent in life. After my mother passed, we settled with our eldest sister, (me and my fourth sister), we were still in junior high school at that time. That was a transition for me. Suddenly my life just changed. I learned to wake up early, cook for our breakfast, wash our own clothes, work on our neighbor’s farm just to have an allowance, sometimes we went to school without even a single penny.

Those were the times that I experienced trauma as my sister’s husband is a drunkard man and became violent whenever it happened. Gradually, I learned to accept the set up. I had to adjust as we just lived with them. It’s never easy. Sometimes I just didn't want to go home, afraid of what might happen later at night. I had nothing to do with it. Sometimes I just asked God why it happened but every season has a reason and a lesson. Gradually, I realize that life is survival. It just depends on how you handle every situation that comes.

Until I pursued my senior high school, the set up was still the same. Sometimes, I had to sacrifice my allowance just to ride a motorcycle to go home as my brother-in-law couldn’t pick me up because he’s drunk or just didn’t want to. I had to work every weekend to have my allowance and for my projects and other school fees. Yet I survived. Despite everything, my sisters help me and support me in my whole journey. I graduated senior highschool and I am just very happy about that milestone. It felt like I have already proved and achieved something. And it's just something to be proud of. Truly God will never forsake us.

During my 12th grade, Passerelles Numeriques came to our school and informed us about the scholarship that they offer. I had the urge to pursue it and fortunately, with God’s grace, I got qualified. Actually, I just wanted to find a job if ever I wouldn’t be qualified for PN as given that we are financially unstable, I can’t afford to go to college. Luckily, on the 16th day of March, it was just the best birthday gift ever, Miss Irish called me saying “Welcome to PN, Julecenio”. I was so happy, it's an opportunity, a big opportunity. Although the course they offered isn't my first choice, I am willing to learn and I am ready to explore. I remember during our 11th grade in our ICT subject, whenever we asked our teacher about some computer stuff, he just said “explore”. And yes, I am here to explore more. Furthermore, I am here for my dreams, for my family. They are the reasons for this. Whenever I look at their/our situations, I just find more reasons to achieve my goals, to keep going. They are my motivations for this journey. I left home with uncertainties about this track but one thing is for sure, this is for the sake of my biggest goal, to help my family out of the pit of poverty. Now, I am here and I am willing to take all the risk just to achieve it.

Life is unpredictable. Indeed, nothing in this world is constant but change. Some things may not always be what we hope to be. Life is not always what we look up for. It changes every single move we make. Thus, every moment is valuable. We just have to grab the opportunity to express love, gratitude, and compassion to our loved ones when we are still with them before it's too late. We just have to value what we have before time pushes us to value what we had. Lift up everything to God. Dream more and make it happen.
