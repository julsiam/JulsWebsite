"Great Lessons"

Life is our greatest teacher. It teaches us things we can’t learn from the book.

1. In this exhausting world, you will always have your family to lift you up, to fill your present with hope, to provide your soul with motivation and to support your heart with unending and unconditional love. We can go wherever we want, we may settle in different directions but we will always go back to the roots where we started, FAMILY!

2. In this world, no one stays by your side forever. It’s tough but it is what it is. So you have to learn doing things alone. Never depend on others. BE INDEPENDENT!

3. In this changing world, remember that it’s not you versus the other people. It should be you versus the person you were yesterday. Don’t spend your energy trying to compete with others. You are crossing different paths and you shouldn’t depend yours on them. MIND YOUR OWN BUSINESS!

4. In this world where imperfection is a big deal, remember to let go of the idea on impressing others. Never beg for attention and never fit in just to be accepted. As long as you know who you are and what makes you happy, it doesn’t matter how others will see you. You are unique on your own different way.

5. In this world where nothing is constant but change, you should learn to appreciate what you have, before time pushes you to appreciate what you had. Spend your time wisely with the people you love. You don’t know, your time with them is already limited. BE APPRECIATIVE!

6. In this world where challenges and sufferings exist, remember to always look for the brighter side. “Everything happens for a reason”. At the right time, your heartbreaks will turn into breakthroughs. Always be positive. CLAIM IT!

8. Accept! Move on! Let go! Let God!...Life goes on!

"She's Gone"

I was walking home when someone called my name. As I turned back, I saw how terrified he was. He was anxious and disturbed. I asked him what's the matter and what he told me were enough to stop my senses. After hearing those words, I felt a bang in my chest. A fire started to burn inside me that drove to the arise of different emotions. There was a moment of deafening silence and I couldn't move. My world just ubruptly stopped for an instance. My mind wasn't able to process anything. After a while, I found myself running as fast as I could after the moment of surprise. I was chasing my breath. I didn't know what to do. I was like a poor rat chased by a lion. When I arrived home, I could sense the mourning of everyone. It felt like a storm just passed by, wiping everything in its way. Something unexplainable happened. I felt suffocated, still chasing my breath. My feet didn't want to take a step. Different emotions came in and I couldn't help it. But there's no turning back. I was there already, so I continued. Although I was in a complete chaos, I managed to enter the room. From there, I couldn't stop my tears from bursting as I saw the woman on the corner lying on a rough wooden bed. There's this unfathomable expression on her face and the coldness enwrapped her being. I gave her a tight grip and from that moment, I realized something. I just lost the most important person in the world. She's my mother lying in bed, lifeless. After what happened, it felt like a catastrophe. Indeed, nothing in this world is constant but change. Life must go on, besides, that's the reality. As you grow older, you will lose people in your journey but what's more important is that you had created memories with them. Memories that will forever be cherished.

"You Also Expect"

You know, sometimes we are in a situation where expectations from our loved ones are way too high to meet and too many to take. Actually I think it's the nature for us people. But lemme me ask you this, is it only the expectations of people around you thats making your life way complicated? Or is it also YOUR EXPECTATIONS FOR YOURSELF? Come to think of it. Don't you set standards and expectations for your own self to reach as well? Sometimes we tend to assume that people's expectations from us are the hindrances that prevented is from our freedom, that limiting us from what we can do, stopping us to expand our horizon and going out from our own shells but have you noticed your expectations for yourself? Not just because there are people expecting it from you that's why you worked hard for it but because of your own standard as well. Did you get my point? Worse is, when you grew up dominating among others, like in everything, in class, in the workplace or wherever, you always got on top, along with the praises, honors, and awards, expectations from people around you and for yourself will also exist. And as time goes by, it will gradually occupy your thoughts. And by that instance, you develop a sense of fear on disappointing yourself and people around you. You are not alone.
